Compare iPhones

iPhone Model Key Features

the iPhone (2007)

The first-ever iPhone was a game-changer. It had a 3.5-inch touchscreen, a 2MP camera, and no App Store—but it set the stage for modern smartphones. Despite its lack of 3G connectivity, it was a groundbreaking device that redefined the mobile industry.

iPhone 3G (2008)

The iPhone 3G introduced faster data speeds and, most importantly, the App Store! This allowed users to install third-party apps, making the iPhone a more versatile device.

iPhone 3GS (2009)

The 3GS brought a faster processor, improved graphics, and video recording. It made the iPhone smoother and more capable, paving the way for future updates.

iPhone 4 (2010)

Apple’s first major redesign featured a stunning Retina Display (326 PPI), a glass and stainless steel body, and a front-facing camera for FaceTime. It also introduced the A4 chip for better performance.

iPhone 4S (2011)

visually similar to the iPhone 4, the 4S featured a faster A5 chip, a much-improved 8MP camera, and introduced Siri, Apple’s AI assistant.

iPhone 5 (2012)

Apple finally increased the screen size to 4 inches, made the iPhone thinner, and replaced the 30-pin connector with the Lightning port.

iPhone 5C & 5S (2013)

The iPhone 5C was a budget-friendly option with a colorful plastic body. The iPhone 5S introduced the Touch ID fingerprint sensor, a 64-bit A7 processor, and a dual-tone flash for better photography.

iPhone 6 & 6 Plus (2014)

Apple finally jumped into bigger screens—the iPhone 6 had a 4.7-inch display, while the 6 Plus had a 5.5-inch display. This generation also introduced Apple Pay.

iPhone 6S & 6S Plus (2015)

With the A9 chip, a 12MP camera, and 3D Touch (pressure-sensitive display), the 6S series was faster and more refined.

iPhone SE (2016)

Apple revived the smaller 4-inch screen with iPhone SE, packing the power of the 6S in a compact and affordable form.

iPhone 7 & 7 Plus (2016)

The iPhone 7 introduced stereo speakers, water resistance, and removed the headphone jack. The iPhone 7 Plus was Apple’s first dual-camera phone, bringing Portrait Mode.

iPhone 8 & 8 Plus (2017)

With a glass back for wireless charging, an A11 Bionic chip, and True Tone display, the iPhone 8 was a polished version of the 7 series.

iPhone X (2017)

To mark the iPhone’s 10th anniversary, Apple introduced the iPhone X, featuring: Edge-to-edge OLED display Face ID (no home button) Animoji & improved dual cameras

iPhone XS & XS Max (2018)

The XS series refined the iPhone X with a Super Retina OLED display, a more powerful A12 chip, and a 512GB storage option.

iPhone XR (2018)

A cheaper alternative to the XS, the iPhone XR featured a Liquid Retina LCD display, a single-lens camera, and a variety of colors.

iPhone 11 Series (2019)

The iPhone 11 featured a dual-camera system with Night Mode, while the Pro models introduced a triple-camera system.

iPhone SE2 (2020)

The iPhone SE2 packed the power of the iPhone 11 into an iPhone 8 body, making it a great budget option.

iPhone 12 Series (2020)

This generation introduced: 5G connectivity MagSafe for wireless charging Ceramic Shield for durability

iPhone 13 Series (2021)

The iPhone 13 Pro models had a 120Hz ProMotion display, better cameras, and longer battery life.

iPhone SE3 (2022)

The third-gen iPhone SE brought 5G and the A15 Bionic chip but kept the old iPhone 8 design.

iPhone 14 Series (2022)

Major innovations included: Dynamic Island (interactive notch on Pro models) Emergency SOS via satellite 48MP camera upgrade

iPhone 15 Series (2023)

Apple finally ditched Lightning for USB-C, introduced a Titanium design for the Pro models, and improved camera zoom.

iPhone 16 Series (2025)

AI-driven features, A18 chip, and enhanced cameras.
